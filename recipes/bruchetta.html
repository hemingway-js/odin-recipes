Bruchetta

Description

Click here to read!

Bruschetta (/bruːˈskɛtə/, /bruːˈʃɛtə/, Italian pronunciation: [bruˈsketta] (listen)) is an antipasto (starter dish) from Italy consisting of grilled bread rubbed with garlic and topped with olive oil and salt. Variations may include toppings of tomato, vegetables, beans, cured meat, or cheese. In Italy, bruschetta is often prepared using a brustolina grill.

Ingredients

  • 2 tomatoes, cubed
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 4 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • seasoning salt to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, mix tomatoes, dried basil, Parmesan cheese, olive oil, garlic, seasoning salt and ground black pepper. Cover and chill in the refrigerator 8 hours, or overnight, before serving.
